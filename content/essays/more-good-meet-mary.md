One of the quiet gifts of a life spent moving is the people you meet along the way. Not the ones you plan for. The ones who just appear — at a dinner table, on a dock, in a conversation that starts about nothing and ends somewhere you didn't expect.

Mary is one of those people for us.

We met her while living on St. John, the kind of place that tends to attract people who have followed something — a feeling, a calling, an idea they couldn't shake. Mary had followed all three. And once you know what she's been out there doing, it's hard not to feel like the world could use a few more people like her.

## 43,000 miles in her mother's old Subaru

From 2016 to 2019, Mary Latham drove 43,000 miles across America. All 50 states. 154 homes. Thousands of conversations with strangers.

She wasn't chasing adventure for its own sake. She was looking for something specific: stories of human kindness. Acts of good that don't make the news. The quiet, everyday moments where people choose each other.

The project was inspired by her mother's words — that there will always be more good out there, if you look for it. Mary took that literally. She loaded up the car and went looking.

What she found, across red states and blue states, in small towns and big cities, in the homes of people who didn't agree on much of anything, was the same thing over and over: goodness. Showing up in the most ordinary places. In the gaps between the headlines.

## A road trip that became something much bigger

More Good Today started as a road trip and became a philosophy, a nonprofit, a book in progress, and one of the most quietly radical things we've come across in years of traveling.

Mary has been featured on the Today Show, MSNBC, the Washington Post, the Kelly Clarkson Show, and Maria Shriver's Sunday Paper — not because she was chasing attention, but because what she's doing cuts through something. In a moment when it's easy to believe the world is only getting worse, she went out and proved otherwise. With a Subaru and a voice recorder and an open door.

The stories she collected are being compiled into a book she plans to donate to hospital waiting rooms — the places where people sit and need something to hold onto. That detail says everything you need to know about how Mary thinks.

## What travel has a way of doing

Here's the thing we've noticed after years on the road: you don't just see new places. You meet people who reframe things. Who remind you what's possible. Who are out there doing something so specific and so true to who they are that it makes you want to go be more specific and more true yourself.

Mary is that kind of person. Her work is a living reminder that goodness is not a passive thing — it has to be sought out, documented, shared. That the stories worth telling are often the ones nobody thought to look for.

Whether you're traveling full-time, taking a long trip, or just trying to move through your regular life with more intention — More Good Today is worth paying attention to. Not because it will make you feel better in a soft, surface-level way. Because it will make you look differently. At the people around you. At the ordinary moments. At what's already there.
