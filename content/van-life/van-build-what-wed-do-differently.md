Eight years ago, Jesse built out our van with his own hands. Every shelf, every wire, every decision about where things would live. It was one of the most impressive things I've ever watched someone do — and we've been living with those decisions every single day since.

We don't regret it. Not for a second. But we've also spent eight years learning what we actually needed versus what we thought we needed. And the van conversion world has changed a lot since we started. Better products, smarter solutions, things that flat out didn't exist when Jesse was cutting wood and running wire in the driveway.

So if we were starting from scratch tomorrow — here's what would be different.

## The fridge

Let's start with the one that gets me almost every single day.

We chose a chest-style fridge — the kind you open from the top — because the logic made sense at the time. Cold air is heavy. It sinks. When you open a top-loading fridge, the cold air stays put instead of spilling out onto the floor. Theoretically, it's more efficient.

In practice, I am constantly digging through it like it's a cooler at a tailgate. Stuff gets buried. I forget what's in there. I pull everything out to find the one thing I need at the bottom.

Jesse would tell you the efficiency argument still holds up. And he's probably right. But after eight years, I'd trade a little efficiency for a regular front-opening fridge I can actually see into. Something small, clean, and easy. We'd go with a quality compressor fridge this time — something purpose-built for van life rather than adapted from a camping setup.

## The kitchen

One thing I'd add that's so simple I can't believe we haven't done it — something to cover the sink when it's not in use. An extra bit of counter space in a van is genuinely valuable, and a well-fitted cover over the sink gives you that without any real tradeoff.

I love our Ninja Crispi. But I'd seriously consider a small permanently mounted toaster oven next time. Something that doesn't have to come out, get set up, get put away. Just lives there and works.

## Solar and power

When we built, we got what made sense for the budget and what was available. It worked. It still works. But solar panel technology has moved fast, and what you can get today for the same money — or less — is genuinely better. More wattage, more efficiency, smaller footprint on the roof.

If we were doing it again, Jesse would go bigger from the start. More wattage than you think you need, because you will always find ways to use it. The days you're parked in partial shade, running the fan all night, charging everything at once — those are the days you wish you had planned for more capacity.

## Power outlets

Add more than you think you need. We'd add more than we have now. And this time, every outlet would have USB-C ports built in. Not an afterthought — designed in from the start. You will always have more things to charge than you planned for.

## Shelving and storage

We used IKEA for our shelving and honestly, I've loved it. We get compliments on it constantly — the color, the look, the way it fits the space. I'd absolutely do IKEA again.

But I'd do the layout differently. And I'd add all the things that make it actually functional at a van-life level — pull-out shelves in the kitchen cabinets so you can see and reach everything without reorganizing the whole cabinet. Pull-out storage in the back. Self-locking drawers that don't fly open when you take a hard corner.

Jesse built everything incredibly well — but we also had to customize as we went. A van build isn't a flat-pack situation. You're fitting cabinetry into a curved, irregular space, and no matter how well you plan, there's always a point where something has to be adapted. We'd go in knowing that from the start and building the flexibility in on purpose.

And the back doors — we'd put exterior gear racks on them. Hanging space on the outside for things you need to access quickly. Wet gear, bikes, straps, whatever you grabbed last and need again first.

## Insulation

This is one of those decisions you make once and live with forever. You can't easily redo insulation once the walls are up and the cabinets are in. We'd be much more intentional about it from the start.

Havelock Wool is at the top of the list. It's a natural wool insulation that handles both thermal and sound — which matters more than people realize until they've spent a night parked next to a highway. It's also moisture-resistant and doesn't off-gas, which feels important when you're living in a sealed metal box.

## Walls and finishes

Here's one we didn't even know to think about eight years ago: the walls. In a house, you paint. In a van, most people just go with whatever panel or wrap they chose at the build and that's it for years.

We'd want something with more character. We've been drawn to two finishes in particular — Roman clay and limewash. Roman clay gives you a warm, textured depth that looks like it belongs in a renovated kitchen. Limewash is softer, more aged, almost European farmhouse. They're different enough that we haven't fully landed on one yet.

We'd also use ClickDeco tiles on the walls behind the kitchen. The appeal isn't just the look, it's the modularity. You can swap them out without a full demo. In a small space where your options for change are limited, that flexibility matters more than it sounds.
